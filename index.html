Astroparticle theory

Dark matter, relic neutrinos, and low-energy particle phenomenology.

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at the University of Liverpool. My work spans relic neutrino searches, the use of atoms and molecules as light dark matter detectors, and broader dark matter and neutrino phenomenology.

Research

Three active strands

These categories overlap, and I still actively work on all three. They are a useful way to organise the current programme rather than strict divisions.

Relic neutrino searches

This strand focuses on how the cosmic neutrino background might be probed in realistic experiments, including coherent scattering and accelerator-based ideas.

The central aim is to understand where genuine detectability might emerge once the particle physics and experimental scales are treated carefully.

Papers

  • Limits on the cosmic neutrino background JCAP · 2023
  • Updated detection prospects for relic neutrinos using coherent scattering JCAP · 2021
  • Relic neutrinos at accelerator experiments Phys. Rev. D · 2021
  • Cosmic neutrino background detection in the minimally extended Standard Model JCAP · 2025

Atoms and molecules as light dark matter detectors

More recently, I have been working on atomic and molecular targets for light dark matter detection. This includes both the phenomenology itself and the numerical machinery needed to compute the relevant response functions with enough speed and precision to be useful.

That effort is what sits behind both the hydrogen work in CINCO and the more recent SCarFFF code.

Papers

  • Generalised hydrogen interactions with CINCO: a window to new physics JHEP · 2024
  • A Numerical Method for the Efficient Calculation of Scattering Form Factors Preprint · 2025

Dark matter and neutrino phenomenology

A broader ongoing strand concerns dark matter and neutrino phenomenology more generally, especially light dark matter. This includes low-energy probes, effective interactions, beam constraints, and ways of connecting formal calculations to realistic searches.

In practice this strand overlaps strongly with the other two, but it is the best home for work that is less target-specific and more generally phenomenological.
